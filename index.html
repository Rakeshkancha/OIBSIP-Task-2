Dr. APJ ABDUL KALAM

Missile man of India & people's President

Here's a time line of Dr. Abdul Kalam life:

  • 1931 - Born on 15 october in Rameshwaram, Madras, India
  • (1954) - Graduates in physics from saint joseph's college tiruchirapalli. He moves to madras for further education."
  • (1960) - Joins DRDO as scientist He completes his degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology(MIT) and joins DRDO as scientist "
  • (1969) - Moves to ISRO kalam moves to Indian Space Research Organisation(ISRO) where he helms India's first satellite Launch vehicle(SLV-III)
  • (1980) - Leads India's entry in the space club ads India's efforts to enter the space club by putting the Rohini Satellite in the Earths orbit with the first indigenous SLV-III
  • (1980-1990) - Develops PRITHVI and AGNI missiles As the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, he is credited with the development and operationalisation of Agni and Prithvi missiles
  • (1981) - Recieves the PADMA BHUSHAN Kalam recieved the padma Bhushan for his contribution to the field of science and Technology
  • (1990) - Honoured with PADMA VIBHUSHAN Kalam is Awarded with Padma Vibhushan, India's Second Highest Civilian honour
  • (1992-1999) - Appointed India's Chief Scientific Adviser Kalam is appointed as the chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the DRDO
  • (1997) - Honoured With the BHARAT RATNA Kalam is awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's Highest Civilian honour for his Contribution to the Scientific Research and modernisation of defence technology in india
  • (1999-2001) - Serves as India's Chief Scientific Adviser Sereves as Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India
  • (2002-2007) - Elected as INDIAS 11th PRESIDENT Kalam is elected the 11th President of india succeeding KR Narayan.He won the 2002 presedential Election with an Electoral vote of 922,884.During his five years in office he was fondly called as 'The People's President'
  • (2012) - Launches 'What can I Give Movement Kalam launches the 'what can I givw Movement', a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption.
